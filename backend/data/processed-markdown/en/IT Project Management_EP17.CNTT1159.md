# 2. COURSE DESCRIPTIONS

The course aims to provide students with basic knowledge of **IT projects, focusing on project management and IT project management**. Students will be instructed to learn and practice management skills. Include sections: project goal; project initialization; processes and areas of knowledge needed to successfully manage information technology (IT) projects. The main functions of management are mentioned in this course such as planning, organizing, leading, controlling, closing, and evaluating IT projects. Tools within software and templates that support IT project management will be introduced in this course to provide effective approaches to managing IT projects.

Course content includes:

* Overview of IT project management
* Conceptualizing and Initializing the IT Project
* Business Case &Project Charter
* Planning and Estimate IT Project
* Work Break Structure
* Time, cost estimate
* Organizing and Executing IT Project
* Development Team project
* Controlling the progress of IT Project
* Controlling the quality of IT Project
* Controlling communication of IT Project
* Controlling the risk of IT Project
* Closing and evaluating IT Project

The course also aims to train thinking skills, the ability to self-study knowledge, and the use of apps to support management projects by working independently as well as in groups. Students will be divided into groups to learn deeply about projects, from which they can show their skills in phases to manage a particular IT project.

# 8. COURSE REQUIREMENT & EXPECTATION

## 8.1. Requirements for taking final /terminal exams

* Students are allowed to take the final exam if their attendance score reaches 5 points or more (on a scale of 10).

## 8.2. Requirements for attending classes

* Students are responsible for attending all classes. In case of absence from school due to force majeure reasons, sufficient and reasonable proof must be provided. For each absence, 1 point will be deducted 1 point. Students who miss any classes more than 3 times, with or without reason, will be considered as failing to complete the course and have to re-register.
* Students will be awarded points for constructive comments these points are added to attendance points and group assignments.
* Groups who do not submit the group work will receive a score of 0 (zero). Late submissions will be deducted for each day of late submission.

## 8.3. Requirements for in-class behavior

* The course is conducted on the principle of respecting learners and lecturers. Any behavior that affects the teaching and learning process is strictly prohibited.
* Students must come to class on time. Students who are late more than 10 minutes after class starts will not be allowed to attend the class. Do not make noise and affect others during the learning process.
* Laptops and tablets are only used to take notes for and calculate for lectures and exercises, absolutely forbidden to use for other purposes.
